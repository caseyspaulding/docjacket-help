# Email Automation

Link email templates to tasks so the right email is ready at the right point in the workflow. Task email automation can prepare an email for review, send an email automatically, schedule an email relative to a task due date, and include configured recipients or documents.

:::tip You're always in control — nothing sends on its own
Worried the system might email a client something you didn't want? It won't. By default, automations **draft** the email and hold it in your **Review queue** for one-click approve, edit, or skip — a client or agent never hears from DocJacket until you say so. Sending automatically is strictly opt-in, per email. You can also turn on an organization-wide safety switch so *every* automated email waits for review (see [Review before sending](#review-before-sending) below). This "draft-and-review" habit is how our most active coordinators send hundreds of emails with near-zero mistakes.
:::

Most task email automation starts in a checklist template, then becomes editable transaction-specific automation after the checklist is applied. Use templates for repeatable workflows; use the transaction task drawer when a real deal needs an adjustment.

## When a task email fires

An email linked to a task has **two** moments where it can fire. Understanding these two moments explains almost everything about task email behavior.

| Moment | When it happens | What it does |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Its date arrives** | The email's own date — the task's due date (or auto-send date) plus any offset — is reached | Depends on the email's [delivery mode](#delivery-mode): prepare the email, or just notify you |
| **You complete the task** | You check the task off | Prepares the email for your **Review queue** |

The same linked email carries both. You don't choose one or the other — you configure the email once, and set its date and its delivery mode.

For each linked email you can configure:

- Email template
- Offset and anchor — such as 3 days before the task due date, or on a Key Date like Closing
- Send time
- Weekend or holiday fallback rule
- Delivery mode — draft the email, or just notify you
- Whether it waits for review before sending
- Recipients
- Attached documents

Example:

- Task: Confirm closing appointment
- Due: Closing Date - 3 days
- Linked email: Send closing reminder 1 day before the task due date at 8:00 AM

Emails prepared this way send from your connected Gmail or Outlook account when one is available. Upcoming or held date-based emails appear in the Review queue's **Scheduled** tab, where they can be edited, sent now, or cancelled when supported.

## Delivery mode

Every linked email has a **When this fires** setting that controls what happens **when its date arrives**:

- **Draft email for review** (the default) — DocJacket prepares the email and puts it in your **Review queue** to approve, edit, or skip. This is what most emails should be.
- **Just notify me in-app** — DocJacket posts an in-app notification telling you the milestone hit, and **prepares no email at all**. Nothing lands in your Review queue.

Choose **Just notify me in-app** when the date is only a heads-up for *you* — "the inspection deadline is coming up" — and you don't want a draft email to clear off your queue every time. Coordinators who review their whole pipeline each morning often prefer this for deadline milestones.

:::info Notify mode only affects the date — completing the task still prepares your email
This is the part worth remembering. **"Just notify me in-app" changes what happens on the email's date. It does not change what happens when you complete the task.** Check the task off and the email is still prepared for your Review queue, ready to send — even if the notification already went out that morning.

So a task like "Title commitment received" can quietly notify you when the date passes, and still give you the email to send the moment you actually complete it.
:::

### Where to set it

You can set the delivery mode in two places:

- **On a checklist template** — open the checklist, expand the task's reminder, and use **When this fires** under the timing controls. Every deal created from that checklist inherits your choice. Set it here when it should apply to all future deals.
- **On a single deal** — open the transaction, click the task, and use **When this fires** in the **Task emails** section of Task Details. This changes only that one deal.

### Reading the status on a task

In the **Task emails** section, each linked email shows what has already happened to it:

- **Sent {'{date}'}** — the email was prepared and sent.
- **Notified in-app {'{date}'} — not sent** — this was a notify-mode email. You got the in-app heads-up on its date; **no email was sent**. You can still send it yourself with **Review & send**, and completing the task will prepare it too.

If an email has already been sent, its **Review & send** button goes away — there is nothing left to send. Notify-mode emails keep the button, because a notification isn't an email.

## Configuring automation in a checklist

Use checklist templates when the same task should prepare or schedule the same email every time the workflow is applied.

1. Go to **Templates > Checklists**
1. Open a checklist in **My Templates**
1. Open the **Tasks** or **Automations** view
1. Select the task that should trigger the email
1. Add the email reminder
1. Choose the email template
1. Set the timing — offset, anchor date, and send time
1. Set **When this fires** — *Draft email for review* or *Just notify me in-app*
1. Set recipients and document attachments
1. Save the checklist

Shared system checklists are read-only. Copy a shared checklist before adding or changing email automations.

When the checklist is applied, DocJacket copies those automation rules onto the transaction's tasks. The copied rules can then use that transaction's contacts, dates, documents, and smart-field data.

## Adjusting automation on a transaction

Use the transaction task drawer when the automation exists only for one deal, or when a template-created rule needs a one-off change.

1. Open the transaction
1. Go to the **Tasks** tab
1. Click the task row to open **Task Details**
1. Open **Task emails** or **Scheduled emails**
1. Link an email template, or edit the copied automation
1. Confirm recipients, attachments, and date anchors
1. Confirm **When this fires** — the [delivery mode](#delivery-mode) for this deal
1. Save the task

Completing a task with review-required email automation prepares the email for the **Review queue**. Setting a date on the email means it can also fire on that date — see [When a task email fires](#when-a-task-email-fires).

Changes made here apply to **this deal only**. To change how future deals behave, edit the checklist template instead.

## Recipients and documents

Task email automation can use:

- To, Cc, and Bcc recipient types
- Contact roles from the transaction
- Direct email addresses where supported
- Document categories or selected document attachments

The linked email template still controls the subject, body, smart fields, and any template-level auto-attachments.

Build and test the template in **Templates > Email Templates** first. The checklist automation controls decide when the email is prepared or sent, while the email template controls the content, default recipient logic, and template-level document attachments.

### Where recipients come from

Recipients can be set in two places, and they work together:

1. **On the task's linked email** — the recipients you set here always win for that email.
2. **On the email template**, under **Recipients by Role** — used as the fallback whenever the linked email has no **To** recipient of its own.

So if you set **Recipients by Role** on a template, every task using that template picks those recipients up automatically. You only need to set recipients on an individual task when that one task should go to someone different.

:::warning "Audience" is not a recipient setting
An email template also has an **Audience** field. That one is for **search and filtering** — it helps you find templates on the Templates page. It does **not** decide who the email goes to. Use **Recipients by Role** (or the task's own recipients) to set actual recipients.
:::

If no **To** recipient can be resolved — for example the role has no contact assigned on that deal — DocJacket does not send the email to the wrong person or drop it silently. It parks a card in your **Review queue** asking you to choose a recipient, and notifies you. Add the recipient on the card and it becomes sendable.

## Review before sending

You control whether an automated email waits for your approval or sends on its own, at two levels:

- **Per email (the default).** Every on-completion email has a **Require review before sending** switch, and it's **on by default**. While it's on, completing the task drafts the email into your **Review queue** instead of sending it. Uncheck it only when you want that specific email to send automatically.
- **Organization-wide (the safety net).** In **Settings > Organization**, turn on **Review auto-emails before sending** to force *every* automated email across your account to wait in the Review queue for one-click approve, edit, or skip — regardless of the per-email setting. This is the recommended setting if you're new to automation or simply never want an email leaving without a final human look. Drafts auto-expire after your retention window if you don't act on them.

Either way, the promise is the same: **nothing reaches a client or agent until you approve it.** Automation does the tedious part — picking the template, resolving recipients by role, filling smart fields, attaching documents — and leaves the send button to you.

## Runtime review

When the checklist is applied to a transaction, DocJacket creates the tasks and their email automation rules. Before sending or parking a draft for review, DocJacket renders smart fields against that transaction.

If data is missing or a placeholder is unrecognized, review the email before sending. Missing transaction data should be fixed on the transaction. Unrecognized placeholders should be fixed in the email template.

Review-required emails appear in the topbar **Review queue** under **Ready for Review**. Scheduled emails appear under **Scheduled** when they are waiting, held, or ready for schedule management. Nothing sends from the review-required path until a user approves, sends, skips, or dismisses the prepared email.

## Tips

- Use **Require review before sending** for client-facing or high-risk emails.
- Use **Just notify me in-app** for date-based deadline heads-ups you don't want to clear off your queue — you'll still get the email when you complete the task.
- Use **Draft email for review** for anything that should actually reach a client or agent on its date.
- Set the delivery mode on the **checklist template** so new deals inherit it, rather than fixing each deal one at a time.
- Set **Recipients by Role** on the email template once, instead of setting recipients on every task that uses it.
- Test the linked [email template](../templates/email-templates.md) before attaching it to automation.
- Combine email automation with [checklists](./task-templates.mdx) to set up entire email workflows for a transaction type.
