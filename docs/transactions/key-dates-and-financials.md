# Key Dates & Financials

Every transaction has dates and dollar amounts that need to be tracked carefully. DocJacket keeps all of this organized on the transaction overview.

## Key dates

The dates section on the overview shows your most important deadlines:

- **Contract date** — When the contract was executed
- **Closing date** — The target closing date
- **Inspection deadline** — When inspections must be completed
- **Appraisal deadline** — When the appraisal is due
- **Financing deadline** — When the buyer's financing must be secured
- **Other custom dates** — Any additional dates relevant to the deal

These dates stay in sync with your timeline milestones. If you change the closing date, DocJacket recalculates all the downstream deadlines automatically.

<!-- Screenshot: key dates section on overview -->

### Editing dates

Click any date field to edit it. When you change a date that other milestones depend on (like the closing date), DocJacket will ask if you want to recalculate the rest of your timeline.

## Financial summary

The financials section gives you a quick snapshot of the money side:

- **Purchase price** — The agreed sale price
- **Earnest money** — The deposit amount and who's holding it
- **Loan amount** — If the buyer is financing
- **Down payment** — Calculated from price minus loan amount
- **Commission** — Agent commission details
- **Other fees** — Title fees, closing costs, etc.

<!-- Screenshot: financial summary section on overview -->

### Editing financials

Click any field to update it. If you used AI extraction to create the transaction, these fields are pre-populated from the contract — just verify they're correct.

:::tip
If you're tracking commission splits or detailed closing costs, you can add custom fields in **Settings > Transaction Fields**.
:::
